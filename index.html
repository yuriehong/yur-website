About Me!

Hi! I'm from the Washington D.C. area, where I grew up hiking with my family, playing lacrosse, and exploring coffee shops. I have taken a few computer science courses in high school and college. I decided to take the Flatiron coding bootcamp course in order to solidify good software engineering practices and work with other students on coding projects. I decided to switch from premed to pursure software engineering after working in a clinical setting as a medical assistant and rediscovering my love for coding. I researched in the Santhanam lab at the Johns Hopkins medical campus for 2 years where I studied the fundamental mechanisms of cardiovascular disease. In 2019, my biomedical engineering design team at Johns Hopkins designed a novel one-arm-drive mobile stander for pediatric patients with hemiplegia. Coding languages I am familiar with are Java, Javascript, Python, HTML, CSS, and Matlab.

My Experiences
